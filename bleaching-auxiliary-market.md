<p><p>Bleaching auxiliaries are essential in textile and paper industries, categorized into three main market types: anionic, cationic, and nonionic. Anionic auxiliaries, often negatively charged, enhance the effectiveness of bleaching agents and are suitable for high pH conditions. Cationic auxiliaries possess a positive charge, promoting adhesion and interaction with negatively charged substrates. Nonionic auxiliaries, having no charge, offer flexibility and compatibility with various materials, making them ideal for specific applications like delicate fabrics. Together, these auxiliaries optimize bleaching processes.</p></p>
